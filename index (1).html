What is Liquity

Liquity is a decentralized borrowing protocol on Ethereum. It lets users borrow a stable asset without banks, governance, or human control.
Everything runs on transparent code.

What is $BOLD?

$BOLD is Liquity V2’s stablecoin, backed by ETH and liquid staking tokens like wstETH and rETH.
No banks.
No companies.
Just crypto.
It’s designed to hold 1 $BOLD = 1 USD while remaining decentralized and over-collateralized, meaning every $BOLD is backed by more value than it represents.

$BOLD Stability – How It Works

$BOLD holds its $1 peg through automated on-chain mechanism

Key Mechanics:
• Collateralized Loans: Users lock ETH or LSTs to mint $BOLD.

• Redemptions: Below $1, redeem $BOLD for $1 in ETH, lifting price.

• New Minting: Above $1, more $BOLD is minted, lowering price.

• Stability Pool: Absorbs liquidations to keep system healthy.

What Is Protocol Incentivized Liquidity (PIL)?

PIL = Protocols rewarding users who add liquidity to their pools.
Rewards can be BOLD, ETH, Points or airdrops, Other tokens

Why PIL Matters for BOLD:
1. Better trading – More liquidity means less slippage when swapping BOLD/sBOLD/yBOLD

2. Attracts users – Rewards bring new people into the ecosystem

3. Helps forks launch – New projects can grow communities faster

4. Enables yield strategies – Platforms like Pendle, Balancer, and Morpho benefit from deeper liquidity

5. Strengthens DeFi presence – More liquidity makes BOLD more useful across all of DeFi

PIL grows the ecosystem—users earn rewards, protocols grow faster.
